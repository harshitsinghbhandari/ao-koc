Gojo Satoru and Ryomen Sukuna from Jujutsu Kaisen are deliberately written as narrative parallels / foils — two sides of the same "overwhelming strength" coin. Here are the most commonly discussed similarities:

Both are considered "the strongest" in their respective eras
Gojo = strongest modern sorcerer (the honored one of the current age).
Sukuna = strongest sorcerer in history (King of Curses from the Heian era).
Their climactic fight is literally titled "The Battle of the Strongest".
Immense arrogance and self-confidence
Both are extremely cocky, believe almost no one can truly challenge them, and openly look down on almost everyone else. They acknowledge each other's power but still see themselves as untouchable.
Overwhelming power that isolates them
Their sheer strength makes genuine connections difficult:
Gojo is lonely at the top despite having friends/students.
Sukuna is completely alone because he views everything as beneath him.
Both live as the pinnacle of jujutsu, unable to relate to others.
Mastery of Reverse Cursed Technique (RCT)
Both can use RCT at an extremely high level — healing themselves from near-fatal injuries, regenerating limbs, and even reconstructing their brains repeatedly in combat.
God-like / divine thematic imagery
Gojo's domain hand sign references Indra (king of gods / heaven).
Sukuna's references Yama (king of hell / death).
They're often framed as heavenly vs. infernal opposites.
Unmatched cursed energy volume + efficiency
Both possess absurdly large reserves and near-perfect control (Gojo via Six Eyes, Sukuna via innate talent + experience).
They were waiting / destined to meet each other
Sukuna (in a way) preserved himself for a millennium to eventually face someone on his level — and that person turned out to be Gojo. Their fight feels like fate bringing the two peaks together.
In short: they're mirrors of each other — the strongest of today vs. the strongest of history, both burdened by loneliness born from unchallenged power, both arrogant beyond measure, both ridiculously broken in combat ability.

The entire series builds toward their confrontation precisely because they are so alike in what makes them "the strongest," yet so different in morality and purpose.
